# ClassAction AI — Pain Points & User Problems

## Primary Pain Point
> **"I'm owed money from corporate lawsuits but I have no idea and the forms are impossible."**

## Detailed Pain Point Breakdown

### 1. Awareness Gap
- **Problem:** Consumers have no idea that companies they bought from were sued and lost.
- **Scale:** Over $2.5 billion in class action settlement funds go unclaimed annually in the US alone.
- **Example:** Apple's $500M iPhone battery throttling settlement — most iPhone owners never filed.
- **Why It Persists:** Settlement administrators are legally required to notify class members, but they use the cheapest method possible (tiny newspaper ads, one email that goes to spam).

### 2. Form Complexity
- **Problem:** Claim forms require precise serial numbers, dates of purchase, and proof of ownership that people don't have readily available.
- **Scale:** Even when notified, only 5-15% of eligible consumers actually complete the claim.
- **Why It Persists:** Defense attorneys negotiate intentionally complex claim processes to minimize payout.

### 3. Deadline Blindness
- **Problem:** Claims have strict deadlines (often 90-180 days). Miss it by one day and you get nothing.
- **Scale:** Millions of valid claims expire every year because people procrastinate or forget.
- **Why It Persists:** No centralized notification system exists for consumers.

### 4. Receipt/Proof Burden
- **Problem:** Users need proof of purchase for products they bought 3-5 years ago.
- **Scale:** Most people don't keep receipts beyond 30 days.
- **Our Solution:** AI scans Gmail for order confirmation emails, extracting SKUs and dates automatically.

## Who Suffers Most (User Personas)

### Persona 1: "Busy Professional"
- Age: 28-45
- Income: $60k-$150k
- Behavior: Buys tech, travels frequently, uses dozens of apps
- Pain: "I know I'm probably owed money from SOMETHING but I don't have time to research it."
- Willingness to pay: HIGH ($15 is nothing if the AI finds $200+)

### Persona 2: "Budget-Conscious Parent"
- Age: 30-55
- Income: $35k-$75k
- Behavior: Shops at big-box retailers, bulk purchases
- Pain: "We bought that baby formula that was recalled — are we owed anything?"
- Willingness to pay: MEDIUM (needs to see the found amount first)

### Persona 3: "Data Breach Victim"
- Age: All ages
- Behavior: Anyone who's received a "your data was compromised" email
- Pain: "Equifax/Facebook leaked my data and I heard there was a settlement but the form was confusing."
- Willingness to pay: HIGH (feels violated, wants justice)

## What Users Currently Do Instead
1. **Nothing** (90%) — They never find out about the settlement
2. **Google it manually** (5%) — Spend hours researching, often missing deadlines
3. **Hire a claims service** (3%) — Pay 30-50% of the recovery to a middleman
4. **File themselves** (2%) — Navigate the confusing government forms alone

- **$15 flat fee** vs. 30-50% taken by legacy claims services
- **Automated receipt scanning** eliminates the proof-of-purchase burden
- **Deadline monitoring** ensures claims are never missed
- **One-click filing** removes all friction from the claim process
